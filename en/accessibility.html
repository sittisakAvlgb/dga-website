Accessibility Statement

The Digital Government Development Agency (DGA) is committed to ensuring everyone — including people with disabilities and older users — can access and use this website equally, conforming to WCAG 2.2 Level AA.

  • Fully keyboard operable
  • Screen-reader friendly
  • Adjustable size & contrast

1 Our commitment

DGA believes government digital services must be accessible to every citizen equally — regardless of visual, hearing, motor or cognitive ability. We apply inclusive-design principles at every stage of development and continuously improve.

2 Standard & conformance level

This website follows the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2 at Level AA, covering the four core principles:

  • Perceivable — text alternatives, sufficient colour contrast, resizable text.
  • Operable — full keyboard access, skip links, no harmful flashing.
  • Understandable — clear language, consistent structure, helpful form guidance.
  • Robust — valid HTML and ARIA, compatible with assistive technologies.

3 Accessibility features

  • Adjustable text size (small/normal/large) and zoom to 200% without breaking layout.
  • Multiple display modes: default, dark mode and high contrast.
  • “Accessibility” widget (bottom-left button): highlight links, highlight headings, increase line spacing, big cursor.
  • Text-to-Speech read-aloud in Thai and English.
  • Full keyboard operation with a clearly visible focus indicator.
  • “Skip to main content” link and a logical heading structure.
  • Image alt text, form labels and ARIA live status announcements.
  • Respects reduced-motion preference · bilingual · fully responsive.

4 Technology compatibility

The website has been tested with the following environments to ensure it works with assistive technologies:

  • Latest browsers: Chrome, Microsoft Edge, Safari, Firefox.
  • Screen readers: NVDA, JAWS (Windows) and VoiceOver (macOS/iOS).
  • Keyboard control and operating-system screen magnification.

5 Known limitations

While we aim for full accessibility, some areas are still being improved:

  • Some older PDF documents may not yet be fully accessible — these are being remediated progressively.
  • Embedded third-party content or maps may have a different accessibility level from the main site.

If you find anything you cannot use, please tell us so we can fix it quickly.

6 Assessment approach

We assess accessibility using a combination of automated tools (e.g. axe, Lighthouse), expert review against WCAG 2.2 AA criteria, and real-world testing with keyboard and screen readers.

7 Contact & accessibility feedback

If you encounter an accessibility barrier, or have suggestions, please contact us via the channels below. We aim to respond within 3 business days.

Call 0-2612-6000 Report via complaint system
