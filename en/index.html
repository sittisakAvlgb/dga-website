Q&A

Frequently asked questions

Short, clear answers to the most common questions about DGA and digital government services. Pick a topic below to see the answer.

The Digital Government Development Agency (DGA) is the central agency that develops, connects and elevates public services into digital form — such as the “Tang Rath” app, identity verification, and the government's central data infrastructure — so people can reach services conveniently, quickly and securely.

“Tang Rath” brings government services into one place — check welfare eligibility, pay bills, book agency appointments and view digital official documents. Download it free on iOS and Android, or use it on the web, with identity verified via ThaID.

ThaID is the government's digital identity verification system, used in place of a physical ID card to access online services securely. Register yourself through the ThaID app, or verify in person at a district office.

Agencies can connect through the Government Data Exchange (GDX) platform and DGA's Open APIs by submitting a request via the government-agency channel. Our team coordinates access rights, data standards and security in line with PDPA.

Yes. The site is built to WCAG 2.2 Level AA — fully keyboard operable, screen-reader friendly, with a high-contrast mode, adjustable text size and dark mode, so everyone can access services equally.
